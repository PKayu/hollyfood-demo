The Story Behind the Burger

Our Story

From Chile to South Ogden — built with passion, served with heart.

The Beginning

A Dream Named After Hollywood

Francisco Tobar grew up in Chile with two great loves: cinema and food. As a radio announcer and medical center owner, he built a life rooted in storytelling and community. But the dream of bringing those two passions together into one place never left him.

After immigrating to Utah, Francisco spent two years running a Café Rio location — learning the rhythms of American restaurant life from the inside. Then came the moment he'd been building toward: opening his own place.

He named it Hollyfood — a play on Hollywood. Not just a burger joint, but a cinematic experience. A room where every wall tells a story, every burger has a name, and every guest walks away feeling like they've been part of something special.

"
We want to give people the best experience in their life.
— Francisco Tobar, Founder & Owner
The Atmosphere

Step Inside the Universe

Hollyfood is an eight-table burger bar that doubles as a gallery of pop culture history. The walls are covered with original superhero artwork, movie posters, framed figurines, and comic book panels collected and curated by Francisco himself.

A life-size Darth Vader stands guard. Captain America's shield hangs overhead. Harry Potter and Ghostbusters keep the peace. And two fully-working vintage Pac-Man arcade games are free to play while you wait for your order.

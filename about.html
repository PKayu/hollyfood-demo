The Hero Behind the Counter

Francisco Tobar came to South Ogden with a single mission: to create a dining experience that was about more than just food. A former manager at Café Rio and an immigrant from Chile with a passion for cinema and comic books, Francisco spent years dreaming of a restaurant that would make guests feel like they'd stepped into their favorite universe.

In April 2024, that dream became reality. Hollyfood opened its doors in the former home of Lovee's Cakes — and transformed it into something the Wasatch Front had never seen before.

The Mission: Sell an Experience

"We're not just selling a burger," Francisco says. "We're selling an experience." And he means it. Every square inch of Hollyfood has been deliberately crafted to transport guests into another world — one where the walls are alive with posters, figurines, and memorabilia from the biggest movies and comics ever made.

A life-size Darth Vader stands guard near the front counter. Two working vintage Pac-Man arcade machines hum in the corner. Captain America, Ghostbusters, Star Wars, Harry Potter, and Venom claim every wall. Even the burgers wear costumes — red buns for the American Patriot, dark buns for the Symbiote, green buns for the Incredible Green.

The Craft: Fresh. Always.

The theatrics would mean nothing without great food. That's why Francisco insists on using fresh, never-frozen beef for every single burger. The buns are made in-house, sculpted to match the character each burger represents. The result? A burger that looks like a movie prop and tastes like it belongs in the final scene.

Hollyfood earned a perfect 100/100 health inspection score — a testament to the team's commitment to quality, cleanliness, and craftsmanship. This isn't a gimmick. This is a real restaurant, with real heart, serving real food.
